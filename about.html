My name is Marilyn Lush and I am a qualified and BACP accredited counsellor based in Portsmouth.

Why I Became a Counsellor

I am a fully qualified and experienced BACP Accredited Counsellor and Psychotherapist with an Enhanced DBS Certificate. I offer a warm, supportive and professional counselling service, working sensitively alongside my clients and always at their pace.

I work within the BACP Ethical Framework and am deeply committed to Continuing Professional Development. I see clients at my Southsea or Fareham venues, whichever is most convenient, and I also offer online counselling.

Many people come to counselling feeling lost, low, anxious, angry or overwhelmed. Counselling can feel like untangling a ball of knotted wool â€” gently working through what feels stuck so that clarity, understanding and inner peace can begin to return.

I work by walking alongside my clients as they explore, examine and challenge the issues and emotions that arise. Together, we create a space where you feel heard, understood and supported, enabling you to move forward emotionally and regain a sense of balance and wellbeing.

I work successfully with adults, couples and young people across all age ranges, regardless of gender or sexual orientation.

Qualifications

I hold a Diploma in Humanistic Counselling alongside EMDR Certificates Parts 1, 2 and 3 approved by the EMDR Europe Association. I also hold advanced qualifications in Couples and Relationship Counselling, Counselling and Guidance, and Education.

I am proactive in keeping my knowledge and skills up to date and regularly undertake further training. Recent areas of study include trauma therapy, psychological first aid, emotional resilience, attachment, bereavement and loss, domestic violence, mindfulness and information governance in line with NHS confidentiality guidelines.

I have worked as a counsellor since 2001 and have extensive experience supporting individuals, couples and young people across the full age range.

Accreditations & Professional Standards

I am a Registered and Accredited Member of the British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy (BACP). Accreditation reflects an advanced level of professional training, experience, and a commitment to ethical practice.
